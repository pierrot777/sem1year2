1. What are the 5 elements of an effective group (Dowling et al., 2020)? 
    
    - Clear purpose and shared goals, members understand and commit to a common objective.
    - Defined roles and responsibilities, each member knows what they are accountable for.
    - Effective communication, open, respectful, and timely exchange of information.
    - Trust and cooperation, members rely on one another and work collaboratively.
    - Appropriate leadership and decision-making, leadership supports coordination and constructive decisions.
2. What different ethical theories do Dowling et al., (2020, pp. 143-147) outline?
    
    - Utilitarianism – actions are judged by their consequences; the best action produces the greatest overall good.
    - Deontology (duty-based ethics) – actions are judged by adherence to rules, duties, and obligations, regardless of outcomes.
    - Virtue ethics – focuses on the character and moral virtues of the decision-maker (e.g. honesty, integrity).
    - Rights-based ethics – emphasizes respecting the rights of individuals (e.g. safety, autonomy).
    - Justice-based ethics – concerned with fairness and equitable distribution of benefits and burdens.
3. What are the Engineers Australia Stage 1 Competencies? Who decided on these competencies? How do they relate to what needs to be studied in Engineering degrees in Australia? The competencies were developed and decided by Engineers Australia to align with the international engineering standards and the International Engineering Alliance. Australian engineering degrees are accredited against the Stage 1 Competencies.
    

Stage 1 Competencies

- **Knowledge and Skill Base**
    - Engineering science, mathematics, specialist knowledge, design, sustainability
- **Engineering Application Ability**
    - Problem solving, design, use of tools, project and risk management
- **Professional and Personal Attributes**
    - Ethics, communication, teamwork, leadership, lifelong learning
